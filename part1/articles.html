On Subject Matter Experts

Identifying the "Right" Subject Matter Expert

Jane Bozarth

In Nuts and Bolts working with Subject Matter Experts by Jane Bozarth, She infers that half the battle can be won by identifying the "right SME." According to the article it's important to find someone new enough that can remember what their learning process was like. Far too often in projects you can be paired with an out of touch manager as a subject matter expert. Managers can be important to give you the overall picture, but if your job is to capture details about a process, look no further than the ones getting their hands dirty.

Working with Subject Matter Experts


Jeffery Rohrlick

Another article that resonated with me in regards to an SME interview is How to Dig in the SME Goldmine: Working with a Subject Matter Expert by Jeffrey Rohrlick . In particular the paragraph on collaboration where the author talks about how to help a SME pair down a large presentation. "Help divide the information into 3 categories, Vital to know, Good to Know, and Nice to Know." If there is still pushback or unwillingness to let go of the Nice to Knows, then add it to a separate Resources section.
